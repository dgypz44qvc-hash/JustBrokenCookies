How We Build Stories

The 3D Process
NO. 01

Define

understand

Before we design anything, we take  our time.

To understand.
Who and what we are working with.  
why our work is needed, who is it for,  the real thing , the real people behind the brief.

What is the subject? A person, a brand, a project. What stage is it at? What is working, what is missing, what is unclear.

We ask simple questions, but we go a bit deeper than usual. What do you actually want people to feel? What should change after they see this? Who is it really for?

Then we look at the tools. Does this story need photography, video, a website, or written content? Or a mix of them. The choice comes from the goal, not from trends.

By the end of this step, things are clearer. We know what we are building, why we are building it, and how we might tell it.

NO. 02

Design

craft

This is the building phase. We take what we understood and start shaping it into something real. We design, test, adjust. We keep what works, we remove what doesn't.

We focus on details. How images are composed. How a video moves. How text sits on a page. How everything works together, not as separate parts, but as one story.

We don't add things just to make it look good. Everything needs a reason. If something feels too much, we simplify it. If something feels empty, we build it up.

This is also where different media connect. A visual can support a message. A layout can guide attention. A small detail can change how everything is perceived.

At the end of this phase, the story is clear, structured, and ready to be experienced.

NO. 03

Describe

deliver

This is when the story goes live. It's not just about putting it online or showing it. It's about how people experience it.

We think about flow. What do they see first? What comes next? Where do they pause? What stays with them?

We don't try to say everything at once. We let the story unfold in a way that feels natural.

If it's a website, it guides you. If it's a video, it carries you through. If it's text, it stays simple and direct.

When it works, people understand it quickly, but it also stays with them a bit longer. That's the goal. Not just to show something, but to make it land.
