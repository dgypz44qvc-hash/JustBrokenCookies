How We Build Stories

The 3D Process
NO. 01

Define

understand the story

Before anything gets made, it has to be understood. What is the subject? Who needs to hear it? What should they feel?

We start by sitting with it — the brand, the product, the person. We ask the uncomfortable questions. What are you really trying to say? Who actually cares? Then we figure out the medium: photography, video, a website, a written piece. The format follows the story, never the other way around. Nothing gets shot, sketched, or written until we know exactly what success looks like.

NO. 02

Design

craft the story

This is the building. Mood, tone, structure, rhythm — every creative choice is made here, on purpose.

Storyboards go up on the wall. Shot lists get argued over. Colour palettes get tested against real light. We write scripts, cut wireframes, set type, choose music. The work is slow and deliberate because this is where a story either earns attention or loses it. By the time we leave this phase, every frame, every word, every transition has a reason to exist.

NO. 03

Describe

deliver the story

The story goes live. A film ships. A site launches. A campaign drops. This is where it stops being ours.

Everything converges — production, post, publishing. We deliver across every touchpoint so the message lands whole, not in pieces. The final cut plays. The pages load. The words reach an inbox. And then something shifts: the story belongs to the people who see it, share it, talk about it at dinner. That handoff — from our hands to their memory — is the whole point.
